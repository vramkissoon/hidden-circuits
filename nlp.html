My Story

I fell in love with computer science and programming in the spring of my freshman year. I had enrolled in 'How to Design Programs' with Professor Dan Licata. It was my first programming class ever. I did not know what to expect. Yet, I would be lying if I said I did not expect the class to be incredibly meaningful in some way. Computers, almost as much as literature, underpinned some of my most formative interests. And now I was going to study the art of computation formally. Of course it was going to be meaningful.

Language and math are motifs that reappear throughout my life. More than that, they are perhaps the foundations of my most creative acts.

I had two choices for a final project in Professor Licata's classs. I opted to not build a version of Brick Breaker. Instead, I chose the other option, the one which lay at the convergence of mathematics and spoken language. I am going to recreate it now, and I am going to make it better.
